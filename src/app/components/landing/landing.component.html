Form-based Chart Abstraction

About

Welcome to the SmartChart platform's Form-based Chart Abstraction application.

SmartChart provides a complete platform for multiple client applications to build upon a common backend service built around the HL7 FHIR specification. The platform leverages Clinical Quality Language (CQL) in conjunction with Natural Language Processing (NLP) to gather complete patient health records from a FHIR compliant Electronic Health Records (EHR) and streamline the data to provide clearly defined groupings of evidence for defined concepts.

Concepts in SmartChart

Broadly, a concept is any semantically meaningful piece of data. Concepts may represent real world entities (medications, procedures, etc.) or be more abstract, such as questions about a person or entity ("Is the patient deceased?").

In SmartChart's Form-based Chart Abstraction, concepts are any fields within a form or questionnaire. For example, an item in a reporting questionnaire asking "When did treatment begin?" is considered a concept.

Forms and Jobs

A form is a collection of concepts, typically rendered to users as questions and prompts defined by subject matter experts. Forms may represent common healthcare surveys or questionnaires, reporting workflows, or be entirely custom.

For each concept within a form, CQL or NLP logic may be attached in an attempt to provide the information from the patient's health record. This logic is described as a job to be executed. A job is either CQL or NLP, not both at once, and a concept may be associated with more than one job. The case of one CQL job and one NLP job for a given concept is common.

Where a form is the largest unit of work, a job is the smallest.

Evidence

SmartChart concepts are paired with "evidence". Evidence are parts of the clinical record which contains relevant information to the concept.

When a new patient form is started, CQL and/or NLP logic defined as part of the form and associated with one or more concepts is executed against the patient's health records. The logic will define how to review both structured and unstructured clinical data in an attempt to gather the relevant evidence.

This evidence is then presented to the user in two ways:

  1. As a collection of FHIR Resources which contain the data as it appears in the EHR to facilitate quickly reviewing the patient's health record.
  2. When possible, summarized as a suggested answer for a concept. For example, in addressing the concept "If the patient is deceased, what is their date of death?" a single answer can be inferred from the data. (Note: The evidence collection is still included alongside any suggested answers to allow human confirmation.)
Instructions
1. Select "Form Manager" in the Menu (Click for detailed information.)

In the left side navigation window, select "Form Manager". You will be taken to a list of active patient forms. These may either have logic running in the background or already be completed.

2. Start a New Patient Form (Click for detailed information.)

If you already have form previously started you intend to use, skip this step.

In the Form Manager, select the "Start New Patient Form" tab. You will be asked to select a patient and a form. System administrators may provide lists of pre-identified patients, presented as cohorts, for quick access. A patient may be selected from these lists, or the user may search for a given patient on the EHR.

To search for a patient, select "Patient Search" while in the "Start New Patient Form" tab. You must then select a search type as follows:

  • Identifier - Search by a known patient identifier such as a medical record number or social security number.
  • Name and Date of Birth - Search by a patient's given name, family name, and date of birth.
  • FHIR ID - Search by a FHIR Patient Resource's ID in the EHR FHIR Server. This information is likely not available for all users, in which case one of the previous two options should be preferred.

Once you have selected a patient the form you wish to start for that patient, press the "Create Patient Form" button. This will start a new form and begin running the associated logic to collect evidence.

3. Select an Active Form (Click for detailed information.)

In the Active Forms tab of the Form Manager, you will see a list of all forms previously created. If you have just started a new form, it should appear at the top of the list.

To find a particular form, you may use the "Patient Search" options at the top to filter the list of active forms.

Select the patient form you wish to load.

4. Fill Out Your Form (Click for detailed information.)

Once a patient form is selected, you will be taken to a page in order to review the evidence and complete the form.

WARNING: Before proceeding with completing the form, you should check the status of the form's logic in the header. It will provide a completion percentage as it processes the patient's healthcare record. The running time will depend on the form and logic. As the jobs complete, the page will continue to make new evidence or suggested answers available to users.

As forms may vary, you should follow the instructions presented for each question or prompt. Each may have a suggested answer or evidence gathered through the abstraction process. Suggested answers should be reviewed carefully. You may click on the suggested answer to automatically fill in the form with that answer.

If there is available evidence gathered from the EHR, a "View Evidence" button with a count of the amount of evidence found. Selecting this button will open the evidence viewer panel on the right side of the screen. Structured healthcare record data is presented as a table based on the type of data, while unstructured data such as notes are provided below. The terms used in the logic for the processing of the unstructured data is given, with relevant matches in the source note or document highlighted. You may also open the raw document text in a window for further review. You may filter both types of evidence by dates using the controls at the top of the evidence viewer.

5. Submit Your Form (Click for detailed information.)
